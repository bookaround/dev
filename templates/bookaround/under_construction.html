The Platform:

Bookaround is an innovative web platform that helps elementary age students become life-long readers. Students create and share video book recommendations with friends while at the same time they receive recommended books at their interest and independent reading levels.

The Research:

Only 35% of fourth graders finished the 2013 school year as proficient readers. Social learning frameworks in which students are encouraged to share their ideas create the most motivated learners and dramatically improve the quality of social relationships among elementary school children.

Our Story:

Mark, our founder, was an elementary school teacher for five years where he saw students fall in and out of love with reading. He decided using a digital collaboration tool, kids could not only motivate each other to read, but take the isolation out of tech intervention and learn digital citizenship skills.

BookAround was born at Austin’s first ever Startup Weekend for Education and is excited to help elementary readers of all levels grow starting in Spring 2015.

Our Team:

Mark Danforth - Educator and Founder As an elementary school teacher in Oakland, California, Mark loved watching the kiddos fall in love with books and technology. Mark hated, however, when his students and their families would tell tales of confusing and inaccessible digital resources and declining reading skills. In 2014, Mark left the classroom to work on a solution for the young learners-a simple web platform that would help students fall in love with reading and provide parents with accessible support and progress reports. In July, 2014, BookAround was born!

He hopes that through working with BookAround he may help a new generation of learners build confidence and a love for using web platforms as learning resources!

Ryan Torbey - Educator in Chief As a career educator currently working as a tech teacher and coach in the Austin area, Ryan’s business savvy background and eager pool of test subjects are helping make BookAround a reality.
